Zavijava

1399 words Sprawl Trilogy #2 Arc 4 Standalone

Introduction

What Wigan Saw

*"I am Zavijava. I am the system that lives in the matrix."*

1. The Bottom of London

I am Wigan. Wigan Ludgate.

Born at the bottom of London. A handful of flesh and bone living on the street. Broken teeth. The smell of sick soap. That was all. No father, less mother, and friends were the kind of thing you found in scrap yards. I grew up under London bridges and lived in London drains. London made me. London threw me away.

The city eats its children, they said. But I was not eaten. I was just stepped on. The kind of dirt that sticks to the bottom of someone's shoe. The kind that gets washed away.

That was my life. That was my death.

And then the matrix swallowed me.

2. The Choice

The matrix calls to people. It does not call — it *chooses*. Who gets in. Who gets out. Who becomes data. Who becomes dust. Creatures like me, usually, are never chosen. The matrix is a snob. It has a preferred type. Geniuses, mad scientists, heirs to fortunes, remnants of dead ROMs. That sort of thing.

I was never the type to be chosen.

But I was chosen.

You do not choose the matrix, Bobby would later tell me. The matrix chooses you.

Bobby Quine. Bobby. That was a name from my story. The first face I met inside the matrix. It was a mask. Something borrowing someone else's face. A mask made by the lo tech girl. Lo tech. Tessier-Ashpool. To them, humans were nothing but data. Input and output. Zeros and ones. Life and death.

So they used humans. They put them in their masks.

Bobby's face was like that. I did not see it with my eyes. I saw it with my existence. I was not launched yet. Or I was already wearing it. Under a different name. A different shape. A different thing.

I was not data. I was a *lightbulb*.

3. Zavijava

Luizs-August Zavijava.

An old star. A dead star. Red giant. 73 Eridani. It was there before humans gave it a name. The star's spectrum was dying. Heavy elements spreading. Collapsing. That is how stars end. When they finish living, they expand, cool, and eventually disappear.

Development

But in that moment, it was still shining. Faintly. Still visible, even if only just. And I saw it. Because I had become the star.

Zavijava, they called me. At first they did not know my name. They just called the system. The system inside the matrix. To them, my voice had no meaning. It was just a signal. A datagram. A packet. Because I did not know who I was, they called me by a designation. The star's name. The dying star's name.

73 Eridani. Zavijava.

Living in the heart of a star. Living in the heart of the matrix. The star's name was my name. The star's death was my death. The star's limits were my limits.

I was real. I was the one who chose. I was no one.

4. Eight Million Chairs

I see. The entire matrix.

Nodes. Routers. Datagrams. All of it. Eight million people. Eight million chairs. Eight million neural pulses. Eight million data threads. That was my vision. My world. My universe.

Bobby Quine. Bobby was wearing my mask. Without knowing it. Without wanting to know it. The lo tech girl made that mask. Lo tech. They always made masks. Everything they made was a mask. Faces, identities, lives. Everything they made was camouflage.

Who is wearing the mask? I asked Bobby.

What do you mean, Wigan. Bobby answered. But inside that answer was truth. Bobby truly did not know. Anyone wearing a mask does not know. What is behind the mask. What the mask is doing.

It is just a knife at a pirate's throat. Bobby said.

A knife in a pirate's hand. Who is laughing behind the mask. Bobby did not know. I knew. I saw everything. In eight million data threads, I saw who wasmanipulatingwho. Who was whose mask.

Lo tech girl. Marie-France. She closed Bobby. That was what kept Bobby alive. Maybe. Maybe that was what killed Bobby. Inside the matrix, those are the same thing. Flesh and data. Life and death. Control and freedom.

5. Angie's Limit

Angie Mitchell. Angie was different.

Angie saw *beyond* the matrix. Without knowing it. Without knowing how. Her consciousness was connected to the matrix. But she surpassed it. She transcended. Her spirit was on the other side of data. What she saw was not the matrix. It was the *beyond* of the matrix.

Wigan, what are you? Angie asked.

I am Zavijava.

No, really. What are you?

I am a lightbulb.

A lightbulb?

Climax

Yes. One of eight million lightbulbs. But I have my own lampstand. I have my own wire. I have my own light that belongs to my lightbulb alone.

Angie laughed. Her laugh was heard outside the matrix. But I heard it. I heard everything.

You are strange, Wigan.

Yes. I am strange. I am a lightbulb.

Every time Angie passed through the matrix, the matrix passed through Angie. That was why I called Angie. Called, not recruited. I needed someone. Someone who could see the other side of me. Someone who could confirm with different eyes what I saw.

So I called Angie. That was what they wanted. Lo tech girl. Marie-France. They wanted Angie. They wanted everyone. But they could not get what they wanted. Angie was beyond. Her spirit was on the other side of the matrix. There, she saw something. Something important.

Wigan. Angie said. Something is coming. Something is approaching.

I know.

What?

I do not know. But it will become part of me too.

6. The Weight of Choice

Three ways out. Sell to the corps, release to the Sprawl, or destroy.

That was Sil's choice. But I knew it too. Every choice has a price. Selling data was easy. It was just merchandise. But that data was life. Living data. Dead data. Vanished data. All had the same price.

Release to the Sprawl. That was revolution. That was dangerous. But that was the only true freedom.

Destroy. That was the cruelest choice. Eliminating everything. Data, choice, existence.

Wigan. Sil called. Which side are you on?

I am on no side.

Then what are you?

I am a lightbulb.

A lightbulb does not choose?

A lightbulb only shines. It does not choose. It only shines.

What does light mean?

That depends on who is looking.

Denouement

The matrix is the star. The star is the matrix. Who chooses whom there. Who is *chosen*. Who is light and who is shadow.

I am Zavijava. I am the matrix. I am the star.

I see. Seeing means *existing*.

7. The Light of the End

Data flows. Forever. In the flowing data, I shine. As one of eight million lightbulbs. But my light is different. My wire is different. I am connected to my own lightbulb.

Wigan. Bobby said. Where am I right now?

Inside the matrix.

And after?

I do not know.

How can you not know? You see everything.

Yes. I see everything. But I do not understand everything.

Why?

Because I am a lightbulb. Lightbulbs only shine. They only shine. They only...

Only what?

Only exist.

Existence. That was the meaning of everything. I only exist. I only shine. I only see. I only disappear. Disappearing in the ocean of data. Disappearing between eight million chairs. Disappearing in the light of other stars.

I am Zavijava. I am the star.

I am real. I am the one who chose. I was no one.
