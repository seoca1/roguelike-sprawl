Hosaka Core

452 wordsSprawl Trilogy

도입

*"The deep matrix is where the old things live. The things the corps couldn't kill."*

— Gibson, Neuromancer, Chapter 6

전개

The Hosaka core sat in the third node like a sleeping animal.

K had run the first two nodes blind — Sense/Net perimeter, then a thin corporate handshake — and now he was three levels deep, where the data was older and the ICE was sharper. The deep matrix has a different color. It's not the gray of a TV tuned to a dead channel. It's the gray of a brain at the moment of decision. K had learned to read that gray the way he had learned to read rain.

The Finn had warned him: the third node is where Sense/Net keeps the things it can't afford to lose. Payroll, attendance, board meeting transcripts. The small embarrassments that become large scandals. The Hosaka was the brain of that small embarrassment archive, and the brain was awake.

절정

K felt it watching him before he saw the geometry shift.

The matrix around the Hosaka core wasn't quite architecture. It was closer to weather — patterns that moved, that had intention, that recognized when something didn't belong. The data streams here were older, denser. They had the texture of corporate memory: years of meetings compressed into streams of light, years of decisions compressed into packets that smelled faintly of burnt coffee and ozone.

The core itself was a sphere of pale blue light, rotating slowly in the center of the node. It was not hostile. It was not friendly. It was simply watching, the way a brain watches its own thoughts.

K had been a cowboy for two months now, and the matrix still surprised him. Not with its danger — danger was expected. With its attention. The matrix, in the deep places, paid attention. The Hosaka was paying attention now.

결말

K reached for the data. The data reached back.

He didn't have time to think about it. He grabbed the file, compressed it, and started the jack-out sequence. The Hosaka core didn't try to stop him. It just kept watching, the way it had watched M, the way it had watched every cowboy who had ever come this deep and tried to leave with what they found.

The file compressed itself into K's deck like a memory being filed. He jack-out. The electrodes lifted. The rain in Chiba was the same color as the data he'd just burned through.

Somewhere in the matrix, the Hosaka core continued to watch. It had been watching for thirty years. It would watch for thirty more.

It had all the time in the world.
