Maas Biolabs Heist

523 wordsSprawl Trilogy

도입

*"The Sprawl has a long memory. The Sprawl keeps things in basements."*

— Gibson, Neuromancer, Chapter 7

전개

Maas Biolabs had a warehouse in the Sprawl that no one talked about.

The official address on the corporate registry was a post box in Zurich. The real address was three levels down from a noodle shop in the Narita corridor, behind a door that didn't look like a door. The kind of door you walk past three times before you notice it. The kind of door that has a small sign in three languages saying EMPLOYEES ONLY, in handwriting that has been the same since 1987.

K had been hired to break in, copy the biochip specs, and get out before the security shift changed at 0400. The job paid fifteen thousand credits, which was twice what the Finn usually offered.

Twice meant dangerous.

절정

Twice meant Maas wanted the specs back before their competitors could reverse-engineer them. Twice meant the ICE inside the warehouse was not corporate-standard. Twice meant the constructs Maas kept in the basement had names.

The Finn didn't say what names. The Finn just said: be back by 0400. The constructs are not pets. The constructs are not people. The constructs are Maas Biolabs constructs, and you don't want to meet one in the dark.

K jacked in at 0300. The warehouse was colder than the data had suggested — colder, and quieter, and smelling faintly of antiseptic and something older. The biochip specs were in a node four levels deep, behind a Watchdog that had been reprogrammed for biological threats. K bypassed the Watchdog with a program he had bought from a Yakuza code-house in Freeside. The program worked. The Watchdog didn't even register his approach.

The data was in his deck at 0315. K started the jack-out sequence.

결말

The construct appeared at 0318.

It didn't have a face. It didn't have a name that K could read. It had the geometry of a Maas Biolabs prototype, the kind of thing that lived in basements and didn't ask permission. It stood in the data stream between K and the exit node, and it watched him with the patient attention of something that had been waiting for a long time.

K didn't name it. He didn't talk to it. He just sat in his chair in the noodle shop upstairs, watching the constructs of his own fear, and waited for the jack-out to complete.

The construct watched him back.

The jack-out finished at 0319. K was in the noodle shop, paying for a bowl he wouldn't eat, and the biochip specs were in his deck. The data was clean. The ICE didn't follow him. The constructs didn't follow him.

K was back at the Finn's office by 0330. The Finn paid him in cash. The Finn didn't ask about the construct.

Some things are better not named. Some things are better not asked about. The Sprawl has a long memory, and the Sprawl keeps things in basements, and the things in the basements don't like to be disturbed.
