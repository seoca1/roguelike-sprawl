Sense/Net Infiltration

1165 wordsSprawl Trilogy

*"The matrix is a place where the past is never really past."*

— Gibson, Neuromancer, Chapter 4

도입: The Rings

Sense/Net had three rings.

The outer ring was Wisp-class — polite, brittle, the kind of ICE that broke when you hit it hard enough. K had punched through that ring on his first run, walked away with payroll data he never should have touched. The data hadn't been valuable, but the run had been easy, and easy runs were how cowboys got cocky. The Sense/Net security had flagged his trace, logged his deck signature, and let him walk away thinking he'd gotten away clean.

He hadn't gotten away clean. He'd gotten away lucky.

The second ring was different. The second ring remembered.

The Finn had warned him: Sense/Net doesn't forgive twice. They hire out their counter-intrusion to a Maas subsidiary, and the Maas people don't negotiate. The contract comes with a kill clause — your trace gets flagged, your deck gets blacklisted, your jockey license burns in a corporate server farm in Chiba. Your name gets added to a list that every fixer in the Sprawl checks before hiring.

K took the job anyway. Five thousand credits for a market research file — nothing exotic, nothing that mattered. He'd be in, out, gone before the second ring knew he was there.

He'd told himself that three times now. He was starting to believe it.

전개: The Smell

He was wrong.

The second ring was waiting. It had his old trace baked into its patrol routes — the signature of every deck, every program, every coffee he had spilled on his keyboard during that first run. The Sense/Net ICE didn't read his approach. It remembered it. The Wisp at the perimeter didn't even bother with the polite pretense of resistance. It just opened, like a door, and let him through, and then closed behind him.

The second ring was smaller. Quieter. The geometry of the data was tighter, denser — Sense/Net's competitive intelligence, the files that decided who bought who. K navigated by smell. The matrix smells like ozone and old coffee. The second ring smelled like ozone and old coffee and something else — a faint electrical signature that wasn't his.

M.

She had been here too. Three weeks before him, on a job nobody would explain. She had touched the second ring's heart. She had left her trace on the watchdogs. And the watchdogs had remembered her signature — and now they were hunting it.

The watchdogs weren't hunting K, exactly. They were hunting the ghost of someone who had been here before. They were running patterns through their memory, looking for a trace that didn't quite match any of the people who were supposed to be here. They were looking for M.

But M wasn't here. M was gone. M was somewhere in the Sprawl, doing something else, being someone else. The watchdogs didn't know that. The watchdogs only knew what they remembered, and what they remembered was a trace that hadn't been there in three weeks.

Until K walked in.

절정: The Watchdogs

K could feel the watchdogs turn.

Two of them, black-coated, corporate-logic, the kind of ICE that doesn't negotiate. They weren't hunting M anymore. They had found K.

The second ring folded around K like a net. The data geometry tightened. The corridors narrowed. The smell of ozone grew sharper, older, the smell of a corporate data archive that had been waiting for someone to come back.

K's fingers hit keys that weren't there, in a room that wasn't real, chasing a heartbeat that wasn't his. The rings of corporate memory pressed against him, three concentric walls of forgotten ICE.

The watchdogs moved. K ran. The chase lasted twenty-three seconds, in matrix-time, which felt like twenty-three hours. K had never been hunted like this. The watchdogs knew the second ring the way he knew his own deck — every shortcut, every hiding place, every way around. They weren't guessing. They were reading his fear.

The market research file was three nodes away. K could see it — a small packet of data, glowing in the matrix like a single star. He had to get there. He had to copy it. He had to get out.

The watchdogs cut him off at the first node. He pivoted. The second node was a side corridor he hadn't known about. He pivoted again. The third node was the file.

The watchdogs closed in.

K jack out. Not because he had what he came for. Because the alternative was being trapped in the second ring for the rest of his life, hunted by watchdogs that wouldn't stop until they had his trace.

The matrix dissolved. The Chiba rain hit his face. The Ono-Sendai electrodes lifted from his scalp.

The market research file was still in the second ring. M's signature was still pulsing in the watchdogs' memory. K was alone in a coffin hotel in the 11th level, with five thousand credits he'd earned for a job he hadn't finished, and the knowledge that Sense/Net had his trace.

결말: The Memory

The Finn called three hours later.

"You jack out."

"The watchdogs had me. I couldn't get the file."

"I know. The watchdogs are hunting someone else. Someone who was there three weeks ago."

K was quiet for a long moment. "M."

"Don't say her name. Don't say any name. Just tell me what you saw."

"I saw her trace. Her signature. The watchdogs have been hunting her pattern ever since."

"And now they have yours."

The line went dead. The rain kept falling. K sat in the coffin hotel, watching the rain, thinking about a matrix where the past is never really past. The rings of memory closed around him like the inside of a watch.

The watchdogs had his trace. The watchdogs had M's trace. The watchdogs would keep hunting both, until they caught one or the other. K didn't know where M was. K didn't know if M was still alive. K only knew that the watchdogs had his number now, and the Sprawl didn't forgive twice.

Some things in the matrix are patient. Some things wait. The watchdogs could wait.

K closed his eyes. The watchdogs waited.

Outside, the Chiba rain drummed on the coffin-hotel's corrugated roof — a sound like static, like the matrix's old white noise, like the memory of every run K had ever lost. The ozone smell from the wet pavement drifted up through the vent. Somewhere in the corridor, a vending machine hummed, dispensing amphetamines to runners who couldn't sleep.

K lit a cigarette he didn't really want. The smoke tasted like ozone and old coffee. Like the matrix. Like M.

The Sprawl's rain kept falling. The matrix kept its memories. The past, in the matrix, was never really past.
