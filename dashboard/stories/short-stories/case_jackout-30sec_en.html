Thirty Seconds After

1233 wordsSprawl Trilogy

*"The sky above the port was the color of television, tuned to a dead channel."*

Countdown

Thirty seconds.

Even as the Ono-Sendai Cyberspace 7 electrodes lifted from my scalp, my fingers were still typing. Hands on keys. Hands shaking. Back against the chair. The matrix was gone, but the matrix was still there. Point three seconds of sync between zeros and ones. Less time than it takes a neural pulse to cross a synapse.

The countdown ran in my head like a mantra. Twenty-nine. Twenty-eight. The Ono-Sendai separating from the chair, but the matrix was still my body. The keys were not real fingers. They were matrix fingers. Deck fingers. Data fingers.

Twenty-seven. Twenty-six.

The cherry menthol dropped. Like wet vinyl. The rain. No, the air conditioning. Hudson Hostel television still showing the same thirty-second-old static. Parallel lines. Rainbow-shattered data. Where I had just died. Where I had just lived.

Twenty-five. Twenty-four.

The console table in front of me, cables tangled. The Hosaka black case. The screen saver. Neon in the case. Chatto's 24/7 ICE-FREE advertising. Two hours per mainframe. Chat going all day. Eleventh floor in Chiba, not the Hostel, just a cheap office cubicle.

Twenty-three. Twenty-two.

Saliva. Saliva sticking to the roof of my mouth. Because in the matrix there is no saliva. Because in the matrix there is no body. Because in the matrix there is no I. But here, there is saliva. Here, there is a body. Here, there is an I. The matrix ended but I did not end.

The Real

Thirty seconds later, I was human again.

I stood up. Bathroom. Toilet. Water. The mirror showed me someone young. Someone young. Not as young as in the matrix, but young. The Ono-Sendai did not permit an old body. Thirty seconds of young body. Thirty seconds of living body.

The water was cold. Outside the water, cold metal. Door handle. Evidence that I was still alive. And then, what was different about being alive. You could die in the matrix, and wake up thirty seconds later. You could live in the matrix, and jack out thirty seconds later. Thirty seconds of matrix. Thirty seconds after matrix. Both the same rain. Both the same.

Jack. Just jack.

Eleventh floor in Chiba. Not the Hostel. Rain pouring through the ceiling. Thirty seconds ago, the matrix. Thirty seconds later, outside the matrix. Both the same rain. Both the same. Screen saver. Both the same. Case. Both the same. Cables.

I could feel my hands shaking. So I stood up, like I always did. Bathroom. Start again. In the matrix, outside the matrix, I saw the mirror. Saw my face. Confirmed who I was. Every thirty seconds.

Saliva. Saliva sticking to the roof of my mouth. Saliva.

Ah. Thirty seconds.

The Weight

Thirty seconds felt like thirty minutes.

Outside. Exit. Thirty seconds, then another thirty seconds. The rain continued. Chiba was always like this. When the rain stopped, it was not Chiba anymore. Chiba was a city made of rain. The matrix was a city made of data. Both rain. Both data.

I turned the corner. Turned the corner thinking it would be different. Two walls. Two rains. Two. Pigeons. Pigeons in the rain. Chiba pigeons in Chiba rain. Matrix pigeons in matrix rain.

Chest. Case. Chest. Case. Chest.

The Ono-Sendai was in my chest. The Ono-Sendai was always in the chest. The deck. The deck was the heart. The matrix was the blood. Data was oxygen. I was the brain. Even when the matrix ended, I did not end. Not after thirty seconds.

After thirty seconds, my fingers were still shaking.

Chiba rain made Chiba rain. Matrix data made the matrix. Both without god. Even if god made them, god did not know. Chiba rain. Matrix data. Made me, thirty seconds later. Made the matrix, thirty seconds later, through me.

I turned the corner. Turned the corner thinking it would be different. Turned another corner. The rain continued. The matrix did not stop. The matrix did not stop because the matrix was made by god. Things made by god did not stop. The matrix was made by me. Things made by me did not stop. If things made by me stopped, I stopped. Even after thirty seconds.

Jack-Out

My hands were shaking. Even after thirty seconds.

In the matrix, hands did not shake. In the matrix, there was no body. In the matrix, there was no shaking. In the matrix, there was no I. But when the matrix ended, shaking started. After thirty seconds, shaking started. Every thirty seconds, shaking started. Every time the matrix ended, I started.

The Ono-Sendai dropped from my chest. Chest. Chest.

I, outside the matrix, remembered the inside of the matrix. In the matrix, I was I. In the matrix, there was no shaking. In the matrix, there was no saliva. In the matrix, there was no I. But in the matrix, I was most alive. Outside the matrix, I was most dead.

Chiba rain continued. Chiba rain made Chiba rain. Continued.

I turned the corner. Turned the corner thinking it would be different. Turned another corner. The rain continued. The matrix.

After thirty seconds, my fingers were still shaking. After thirty seconds, the Ono-Sendai was not in my chest. After thirty seconds, the rain continued. After thirty seconds, I.

Jack. Just jack. After thirty seconds. Outside the matrix.

Echo

The Ono-Sendai Cyberspace 7 never touched my hands again. Since that day, I have not entered the matrix. Not once. The matrix was there. The matrix was always there. The matrix was made by god. Things made by god.

The rain in Chiba stopped. After thirty seconds. In thirty seconds. Thirty seconds in the matrix was eternity. Outside the matrix, thirty seconds was thirty seconds. Outside the matrix, there was no eternity. Because outside the matrix, there was no eternity, there was I. Because there was I, there was no eternity. Because there was no eternity, there was I.

I was I. Outside the matrix. After thirty seconds.

My hands did not shake. After thirty seconds. The matrix ended, I started, shaking stopped. When the matrix ended, I thought I started. When the matrix ended, I thought.

The Ono-Sendai was not in my chest. The Ono-Sendai was in the matrix. The matrix had the Ono-Sendai. The matrix had me. The matrix had shaking. The matrix had saliva. The matrix had Chiba.

Outside the matrix, there was Chiba.

I, outside the matrix, remembered the inside of the matrix. In the matrix, I was I. Outside the matrix, I was not I. In the matrix, I was most alive. Outside the matrix, I was most dead.

Chiba rain stopped. After thirty seconds. In thirty seconds, I.

My hands did not shake. After thirty seconds.
