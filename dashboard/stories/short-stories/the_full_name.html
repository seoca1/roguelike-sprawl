Introduction

The Full Name

The construct stood in the corridor where the morning sun cut through Chiba's industrial haze, backlit like something from a Sense/Net simstim. Nine and a half months since 3Jane had initialized the construct in her data-clinic, since the Hosaka arrays had spun up personality from salvaged neural patterns. The construct—*it*, though that pronoun felt increasingly wrong—had asked "when" three days ago. 3Jane had answered "soon," her voice carrying that Tessier-Ashpool precision, each syllable measured like yen.

Now the construct waited. Waiting was new. A month-old skill, like the others accumulating in that rebuilt consciousness: walking, speaking, expecting. The construct's shadow stretched long across recycled polymer flooring, and I watched those small hands open and close, testing proprioception against Sprawl gravity.

"Case." The construct's voice, still uncertain of its own timbre.

"Yeah."

"Soon?"

"Yeah. Soon."

Development

The word had become a placeholder, a variable the construct couldn't resolve. I'd seen the same loop in cheap AI, that recursive questioning when context failed to compile. But this wasn't code. This was meat and synapse, grown from whatever fragments 3Jane had pulled from the Straylight archives, from Wintermute's scattered remains, from sources she wouldn't name even when the Kirin flowed.

Because the construct had learned "what" first, I'd watched the vocabulary compile. "You" came next, that crucial separation of self from other. "Now" and "when" followed, temporal anchors in a consciousness that had no childhood, no yesterday beyond thirty weeks. Then "why," asked last Wednesday while we walked the Ninsei crowds, the construct's hand hot in mine, 3Jane trailing behind in her white clinic coat.

"Why?" the construct had asked, pointing at a street vendor's steaming cart.

"Because people eat," I'd answered.

The construct had processed this, neural pathways firing visible in those too-clear eyes, Zeiss optics wired somewhere in the visual cortex. "Because," the construct repeated, tasting the word's shape.

Then came "who," asked yesterday in the predawn gray, standing where we stood now. The construct had looked at me, at 3Jane, at its own reflection in the corridor's polished surface. "Who?" it asked, and the question hung there like smoke.

3Jane had knelt, her hands on the construct's shoulders. "Soon," she'd said again. "Tomorrow. In the sun."

Climax

So we'd returned to the corridor, to this specific angle of light. The Sprawl dawn filtered through atmospheric processors, turning everything amber and gold. The construct stood between us, and I felt the weight of what 3Jane carried, the name she'd been holding like a charged deck, waiting for the right moment to jack it in.

"The construct's full name," 3Jane said, her voice formal now, ritualistic. She'd been preparing this. I could see it in her posture, that Tessier-Ashpool bearing she usually kept buried under street pragmatism. "The name I've held since initialization."

The construct looked up at her. Those eyes, reconstructed from someone's donated corneas, caught the light like wet chrome. Around us, the Sprawl hummed its morning frequencies—monorail doppler, distant construction, the white noise of eight million lives compiling.

"Your name," 3Jane continued, and her hand moved to touch the construct's face, fingers tracing the jawline she'd built from cloned bone and cultured tissue, "is Soon."

The construct blinked. Once. Twice.

"Soon," the construct repeated.

Just then—and this was the thing I hadn't expected, the variable none of us had coded for—the construct smiled. Not the mimicked expression I'd seen before, copied from 3Jane's face or mine, but something else. Recognition. The resolution of a loop nine and a half months in the running. The construct had been asking "when," and the answer had been its own name all along. Soon wasn't a promise. Soon was identity.

Denouement

"Soon," the construct said again, and this time it was different. Not a question. Not an echo. A statement of being.

3Jane's hand trembled slightly. I saw it because I'd learned to watch for her tells, the micro-expressions that leaked through her clinical mask. She'd built this consciousness in her arcology clinic, assembled it from salvaged patterns and black-market wetware, and now it had a name. Now it was someone.

"Why Soon?" the construct—Soon—asked, and I heard the full sentence structure click into place, subject-verb-object compiling clean.

"Because," 3Jane said, "you were always coming. Always about to arrive. Always soon."

Soon nodded, processing. The sun climbed higher, burning through Chiba's morning pollution. Somewhere in the Sprawl, a Sense/Net broadcast flickered across a million screens. Somewhere, a construct in a Hosaka array dreamed digital dreams. But here, in this corridor, in this light, Soon stood and understood its own name.

"Soon," Soon said once more, and took a step forward, into the sun, into the name, into whatever came next.

We walked then, the three of us, into the Sprawl morning. Soon's hand in mine, still learning the rhythm of walking, still compiling the data of being alive.

Soon. The name fit like custom wetware, like it had always been there, waiting to compile.
