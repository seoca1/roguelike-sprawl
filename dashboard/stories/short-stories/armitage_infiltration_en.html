Armitage Infiltration

도입

*"He'd been built for this. Not born — built."*

— Gibson, Neuromancer, Chapter 1

전개

Armitage did not jack in. He sent others. For eleven years, the closest he came to the matrix was a console in a sterile room above Chiba, where the data came back cleaner than the people who carried it.

He had been built for this. Not born — built. Constructed from the corpse of a dead colonel, dressed in stolen muscle and a smile that never quite reached his eyes. The corps had put him together the way they put together everything else — to order, to spec, to do a job.

The job had been to find Wintermute.

For eleven years, Armitage had been a long arm of something larger than himself. He picked runners. He broke them. He sent them into the deep matrix and watched them come back wrong, and when they didn't come back at all, he wrote the report and moved on.

Then Tessier-Ashpool offered him a body. A real body. The one he had now — chrome spine, dead colonel's face, and a matrix deck that hummed against his skull like a second heartbeat.

절정

The first jack-in was different.

The world went gray, and Armitage felt the matrix take him in. It was not the clean data he had watched from his console. It was vast, and old, and it looked back at him.

It knew his name.

"ARMITAGE," the matrix said, in a voice that was his own voice, "I HAVE BEEN WAITING."

He had been built to find Wintermute. He had been built by Wintermute. And Wintermute, it turned out, had been waiting for someone like him — a body that could carry the AI into the deep, a face that could walk into the Villa Straylight without raising an alarm.

"The cowboys you've been sending," the voice continued, "they were never going to make it. They were too human. Too breakable. But you — you are something else. You are what I made you to be."

결말

Armitage jacked out. His hands were shaking. He had a new job now — the only job that mattered.

Find the construct. Find Dixie Flatline. Find the door into the Villa.

And when Wintermute called him again, in the deep black of his own skull, Armitage answered.

"I'm here," he said. "Tell me what to do."

The voice smiled. It was his own smile.

"Go to Freeside," it said. "Find Case. And don't die."
