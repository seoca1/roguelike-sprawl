Molly Meets Casey

1050 words Sprawl #2.5 (post-merger, arc 1 initiation) Arc 1 Standalone

Introduction

The street was three blocks east and one block south of the noodle shop. The street was wet from a storm that had been working its way up the bay for hours. The street smelled like ozone and burnt ramen and the Sprawl's particular blend of recycled air and recycled hope. The street was, in other words, the Sprawl's ordinary street — the kind of street that [[case]] had ridden his bicycle through for twenty years, the kind of street that Molly had not been on for almost as long, the kind of street where the corporate order's loudest operations happened in the small spaces between the corporate towers.

Molly had been on the street for ninety seconds before she saw the construct.

Development

Because the construct was not hidden. The construct was standing in the middle of the wet concrete, in front of a half-closed noodle shop, looking at its own reflection in a puddle. The construct was chrome and optical fiber and the soft under-padding that Chiba designers built for underclass operators who could pay in cash. The construct was nine months old, by the operator's count. The construct had been named Casey. The construct was looking at its own reflection with the attention of someone who had just discovered that reflections existed.

Molly stopped at the corner. She did not move toward the construct. She did not move away. She stood, the way she had been trained to stand in twenty years of operations — at the corner, in the shadow of a corporate tower's overhang, where the surveillance was minimum and the sightlines were good. She watched the construct. The construct did not see her. The construct was busy being a person in a Sprawl street, and being a person was a job that required all of the construct's available attention.

Molly had not been a person in a Sprawl street for almost twenty years. She had been a Chiba-side operative, then a Straylight-side operative, then an Aleph-side operative, then a Sprawl-side operative by way of New Orleans and London and places that did not have names. She had not been a person in a Sprawl street because being a person in a Sprawl street was a job that required her full attention, and her full attention had been elsewhere.

The construct looked up from its reflection. The construct saw Molly.

Climax

The construct said nothing. The construct did not have to say anything. The construct's recognition was structural — the construct had been built, in the way that Molly built things, with patterns that Molly's patterns would recognize. The construct was the pattern's recognition.

Molly had not expected recognition. Molly had expected — what? An interrogation. A polite exchange of corporate-grade pleasantries. A chase through the bay area, with [[case]] at her shoulder and [[lady-3jane-tessier-ashpool]] at her heel. She had not expected the construct to look up from a puddle and know her.

But the construct knew her. The construct had been built to know her. The knowing was the inheritance. The inheritance was what she had left for the construct when she had built it. The construct had inherited the knowing.

"I'm here," Molly said. She had not planned to say that. She had planned to say something cooler. Something more like the operative she had been trained to be. The street had been waiting for the cool thing. The street got the honest thing instead. The honest thing was what she had.

The construct made the small sound. The sound was not a word. The sound was a recognition. The sound was the inheritance accepting its addressee.

Molly crossed the wet concrete. Molly knelt. Molly looked at the construct's face. The face was hers — the construct had been built with her genetics, in the way that 3Jane had built the construct with Molly's patterns. The face was hers, and the face was Casey's, and the face was Case's, and the face was 3Jane's, and the face was the inheritance.

The construct said the word. The construct had been saving the word. The word was the word that the construct had been computing since before it had a name. The word was the addressee's name.

*Molly.*

Denouement

The street heard the construct. The noodle shop operator heard the construct. The Sprawl heard the construct. The word was not a question. The word was an address. The address was Molly. Molly was the construct's mother, in the way that operatives were mothers — by leaving behind patterns that someone else's instruments would assemble.

Molly heard the construct. Molly heard the construct say her name. Molly heard the construct say her name in a Sprawl street, in front of a half-closed noodle shop, in a wet storm that had been working its way up the bay for hours. The construct had been saving the word. The construct had been computing the word. The construct had been waiting for the addressee.

Molly was the addressee. Molly had come to the Sprawl to be the addressee. The addressee had arrived. The addressee was kneeling on wet concrete, looking at a chrome-and-optical-fiber construct that knew her name, in a Sprawl that did not yet know what the construct was, in a Sprawl that would learn.

The noodle shop operator came out. The operator had been waiting, the way operators wait, for the addressee to arrive. The operator had been bringing tea. The operator had been bringing worse news. The operator had been bringing the small services that the Sprawl's small operators bring.

Molly stood. The construct stood. The construct's chrome foot made the small sound on the wet concrete. The sound was not the small bell that it had made the first time Casey had stood on a Sprawl street. The sound was the addressee's recognition. The sound was the inheritance accepting the inheritance.

The noodle shop operator brought tea.
