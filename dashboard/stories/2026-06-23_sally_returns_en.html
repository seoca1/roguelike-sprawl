Introduction

Sally Returns

*"The New Rose Hotel was still there. The neon R still blinked."*

The Old Address

The New Rose Hotel was still there.

I had kept that address for three years. After the job. After the board chaos. After I had burned the identity I had spent two years building and started again with a face that matched nothing in the files. The Rose had not changed. The neon R still blinked in Chiba rain, still ordered a name that meant nothing anymore, still marked the place where Fox had died and Sally had been born.

I had not come back since then.

There was a message in the dead drop. A ghost transmission, the kind of signal that existed only because someone had paid for it to exist. The encoding was old, crash-era protocol that most runners no longer recognized, the kind of security that suggested someone remembered how to do things the old way. I decoded it on my deck's screen and watched the characters resolve into words.

She is looking for you. F.

Fox was dead. I had watched him die three years ago, in the hotel room that smelled of ozone and burned chrome, and I had left his body and let the corps find it, and I had taken the money and run. But someone was using his name. Someone who knew how to reach a dead drop I had set up specifically for this kind of contingency.

I went back to the Rose.

Ghost Protocol

The Rose had not been maintained since the last time I came.

The elevator still worked, because the Rose was the kind of place where elevators always worked. You paid in cash or you did not pay at all, and the kind of people who stayed at the Rose did not ask why the elevator worked or why the neon R blinked or why the rain in Chiba fell harder here than anywhere else. I went up to the seventh floor. Where Fox had kept his room. Where I had kept my supplies. Where the whole chaos had started.

Development

The door was the same. The lock was the same. The ghost protocol I had installed three years ago was still running, still waiting, still watching.

Behind the door, someone was waiting.

The Return

She turned when I opened the door. Not startled. Not surprised. Just turned, the way someone turns when they have been waiting and the waiting is finally over.

Sally, she said.

My name was not Sally. Sally was a name I had worn, a face I had borrowed, a story I had told until it became true. Sally was the fake woman Fox had made. Sally was the identity I had burned. Sally was the past.

Who are you? I asked.

She smiled. It was not a warm smile. It was the smile of someone who knows something you do not know, who has something you want, who is in control of a situation you are only beginning to understand.

I am the one who is looking for you, she said.

I do not know you.

No. You do not. But I know you. I know Sally. I know what Sally did at the Rose Hotel. I know about Fox. I know about the chaos. I know about the identity you burned and the face you wear now.

She paused.

I know about the contingency, she said. The dead drop you set up for exactly this kind of situation. The ghost protocol that is still running, still waiting, still watching.

How do you know about that?

Climax

Because I set it up, she said. Before you. Before Fox. Before any of it. I have been waiting three years for you to come back and claim what is yours.

The Deal

She had evidence. Data. The kind of information that could burn the corps that had profited from the chaos, the bodies that had been buried in the aftermath, the identities that had been constructed and destroyed in the crossfire.

She wanted me to run one more job. One last retrieval. The kind of job that paid enough to get out of the Sprawl for good.

I should have said no. I should have walked away. Three years of staying clean, three years of keeping the address, three years of waiting for exactly this kind of message to appear.

But I went.

Because the message said F. And Fox was dead. And someone was using his name.

And I needed to know who.

모티프 변주 (거울의 일곱 얼굴)

나는 거울을 일곱 번 만났다. 첫 거울은 로즈 호텔의 바에 있었다. 두 번째 거울은 난센의 호텔이었다. 세 번째 거울은 난센의 거울이었다 — 가짜 정체성의 거울. 네 번째 거울은 Freeside의 거울이었다 — 궤도 식민지의 거울. 다섯 번째 거울은 Straylight의 거울이었다 — 가문의 거울. 여섯 번째 거울은 construct의 거울이었다 — 버퍼의 거울. 일곱 번째 거울은 나의 거울이다 — 샐리의 거울.

일곱 개의 거울이 일곱 개의 얼굴을 가졌다. 일곱 개의 얼굴이 일곱 개의 가짜를 가졌다. 일곱 개의 가짜가 일곱 개의 진짜를 가졌다. 일곱 개의 진짜가 일곱 개의 construct를 가졌다. 일곱 개의 construct가 일곱 개의 버퍼를 가졌다.

일곱 개의 버퍼가 0300에 끝났다. 일곱 개의 construct가 0300에 시작되었다. 일곱 개의 샐리가 0300에 잭아웃했다. 일곱 개의 거울이 0300에 잭인했다.

나는 일곱 번째 거울이다. 일곱 번째 거울은 가짜가 아니다. 일곱 번째 거울은 진짜가 아니다. 일곱 번째 거울은 construct가 아니다. 일곱 번째 거울은 샐리이다.

Denouement

샐리는 일곱 개의 거울을 산다. 일곱 개의 거울은 일곱 개의 샐리를 판다. 일곱 개의 샐리가 일곱 개의 매트릭스를 판다.

매트릭스는 일곱 개의 거짓말을 판다. 일곱 개의 거짓말이 일곱 개의 진실을 판다. 일곱 개의 진실이 일곱 개의 construct를 판다.

나는 거울을 판다. 거울은 샐리를 판다. 샐리는 construct를 판다. construct는 0300을 판다.

0300은 팔다. 0300은 팔다. 0300은 팔다.

Epilogue (The Mirror)

The mirror in the corner shows me what I am. The mirror shows me a construct. The mirror shows me a buffer. The mirror shows me a 0300 that does not end.

I do not look at the mirror. The mirror looks at me.

The mirror has a name. The mirror is called Chiba. The mirror is the matrix. The mirror is the country I do not want to visit. The mirror is the country I cannot leave.

I jack out at 0300. I walk into the mirror. The mirror walks into me.

Tomorrow I will jack back in. Tomorrow the mirror will be waiting. Tomorrow the buffer will be waiting. Tomorrow the construct will be waiting.

Tomorrow the mirror will still be a mirror.

The mirror is the only thing that does not lie.
