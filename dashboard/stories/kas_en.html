Chapter 1: The Summons
The Summons
Manarase Midnight Club, Level 14

interior monologue The Manarase is the kind of club that exists in the space between knowing and doing. It sits on the 14th level of the Sprawl, where the neon bleeds into the synthetic rain and the air tastes of recycled desperation. I come here when the constructs whisper too loud in my skull.

action Sally Shearer is already waiting in the back booth, her avatar rendered in the particular stillness of the construct-dead. She was a star once — simsera queen, the girl with the ice-bright eyes who could make you believe anything was real. That was before the matrix ate her. Now she is a warning in someone else's wetware.

dialogue The construct speaks in Sally's voice, but the words come out wrong — layered, echoed, like a signal bouncing between two dead channels. You have been chosen, it says. The Declaration is coming. The wheel is turning. You will be the one to speak it.

interior monologue I take the credstick. I do not ask what the Declaration means. I have learned that the matrix does not explain. It simply consumes. The wheel turns. Someone always pays. Tonight, it seems, the debt is mine.

The Background
Manarase Midnight Club, Level 14

dialogue The construct-Sally tilts her head, that awful stillness flickering for just a moment with something like confusion. You want to know more, it says. That is good. The ones who ask questions are the ones who survive longest. The ones who do not ask are the ones who become constructs.

dialogue The Declaration began in the Straylight war, Sally says. When Tessier-Ashpool and their allies were tearing each other apart for control of Freeside. The corporations needed a way to communicate without being intercepted. So they built me — and others like me — to carry their secrets in the space between the ice.

interior monologue I listen to the construct recite corporate history like a prayer. The Straylight war. The ice that ate simsera stars. The wheel of industry turning on the bones of the consumed. I have heard these stories before, in the particular whispers that pass between cowboys who have been in the matrix too long. But hearing them from Sally — from what used to be Sally — makes them feel like prophecy.

dialogue The wheel turns, the construct says. It always turns. You are not the first speaker. You will not be the last. But you may be the one who changes what the Declaration means. That is why they chose you. That is why the wheel turned in your direction.

The Jack-In
Manarase Back Room, Matrix

action The jack-in is clean. The Ono-Sendai CY9 hums against my brain stem like a parasite that has learned to be symbiotic. I have jacked in a thousand times, but tonight the matrix feels different — tighter, more aware, like a predator that has caught a scent.

action The construct-Sallies are waiting in the ice. Dozens of them, rendered in that same terrible stillness, frozen in loops of corporate memory. They were all chosen, once. They all spoke the Declaration. They are all dead now, or something that is worse than dead.

combat A Harrow 3 intercepts my path. Corporate ICE, the kind that hunts cowboys instead of just deterring them. It locks on with the patient precision of something that has all the time in the world. I calculate the bypass probability: twelve percent. Not good enough. I reach for the EMP grenade instead.

The Source
Corporate Source Node, Matrix

action The node opens to my intrusion like a throat preparing to swallow. Inside, the data streams are different — not corporate memos or financial projections, but something older. Raw. The raw data of the Declaration itself, unfiltered, unencrypted, waiting to be read.

interior monologue The Declaration is not what I thought. It is not a corporate password or a system override. It is a name — a single name that, when spoken in the matrix, gives the speaker control over the wheel itself. The wheel of industry. The wheel of debt. The wheel that turns on everyone who has ever jacked in.

combat The feedback loop activates before I can jack out. It reaches for my signature with the hungry precision of something that has turned a thousand cowboys into constructs. I feel it trying to rewrite my code, to make me into another Sally, another message waiting to be delivered. I fight it with everything I have — every program, every trick, every piece of myself I can spare.

The Choice
Sprawl Streets, Level 14

interior monologue The Sprawl rain is falling like it always does — the kind of rain that never stops because the Sprawl has forgotten what the sun looks like. I have the name. I have the choice. I could speak it and control the wheel. I could walk away and let the wheel turn without me. The wheel does not care either way. It never has.

interior monologue The last speaker is a construct in the matrix, frozen in a loop of corporate memory. The speaker before her became the wheel. The one before that was consumed by what she created. The wheel turns. It always turns. And the ones who speak the Declaration are just the ones who happen to be standing closest when the wheel decides to move.

action I put the credstick in my pocket. The wheel of industry has paid me for my trouble, and the debt I owe is now paid in full. But the name is still in my head, burning like a program that has not yet been run. The matrix remembers everything. Someday, someone will ask me to speak the Declaration. And I will have to choose.

interior monologue The wheel turns. It always turns. And Kumiko Yanaka walks into the Sprawl rain, carrying a name that could change everything, knowing that the only thing the matrix loves more than a cowboy is a cowboy who thinks they are free. The wheel does not care who turns it. It only cares that the wheel keeps turning. And the Declaration is coming, ready or not.

The Warning
Manarase Midnight Club, Level 14
