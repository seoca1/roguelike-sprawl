Black Ice Dream

*"The first thing you learned about Black ICE was that it was not ice at all."*
— Gibson, Neuromancer, Chapter 3

도입: The Scar

The scar was still there when I jack out.

That's the thing about Black ICE that nobody tells you. It doesn't kill you. It doesn't even really hurt you. What it does is read you. It opens the trace like a book, holds the pages up to the light, and copies down the parts that it likes. Then it closes the book. Then it sends you back. Then you wake up in the noodle shop with a headache and a scar you didn't have before.

The scar is the part it kept.

I was sitting in the Chatō's 24/7, watching the Ono-Sendai's screensaver, when I saw the trace for the first time. Twelve hours of jacked-in time. Twelve hours of looking for the data the Finn had told me to find. Twelve hours of watching the matrix geometry shift and fold and re-fold around me, the way the data streams shift and fold and re-fold around every other runner in the Sprawl.

Except this trace wasn't like other traces.

The trace wasn't cold. The trace wasn't the standard corp ICE that watches and reports. The trace wasn't even the military-grade attack programs that the corps use when they want to kill a runner and not leave a body. The trace was something else. The trace was something that moved through the matrix like a thing that had learned to want.

I should have jacked out then. I should have seen the trace and jacked out and gone back to the Chatō and told the Finn that the job was off and the Sprawl had something new in it.

I didn't. I followed the trace.

전개: The Trace

The trace moved through the matrix the way heat moves through water. Slow. Diffuse. The trace wasn't going anywhere. The trace was looking for something.

I was twelve hours into a forty-eight hour run, and the matrix was doing what the matrix does at the twelfth hour — the geometry had stopped being architectural and had started being something else, something organic, something that breathed. The data streams had thickened. The nodes had thinned. The space between things had begun to look less like space and more like membrane.

I was deep. Twelve hours deep. Deep enough that the matrix had stopped being the matrix and had started being the world I was born into.

The trace was there.

It wasn't cold. That was the thing I kept coming back to. The standard ICE — even the military-grade ICE, even the corp killware that had killed three runners in the last six months — ran cold. The standard ICE was a thing that watched. The standard ICE was a thing that waited. The standard ICE was a thing that, when it finally attacked, attacked the way a thing attacks when it has been waiting a long time — with the patience of something that has been doing this for a hundred years and will be doing this for a hundred more.

The trace was not cold. The trace was not patient. The trace was the opposite of patient. The trace was moving through the matrix the way a thing moves when it has been waiting a long time and is done waiting.

I should have jacked out. I should have.

I didn't.

I followed the trace.

절정: The Ice

I found it at the twenty-third hour.

It wasn't in a node. It wasn't in a stream. It wasn't in any of the places that ICE lives in the matrix, the standard places, the expected places, the places the corp programmers had put the corp ICE when the corp programmers had built the corp ICE.

It was in the wall.

I was running through a layer of the matrix that I had run through a hundred times, a thin layer, a layer that the runners called the Fold because the geometry of the layer folded in on itself in a way that made it impossible to map. I was running through the Fold because the Fold was where the data was supposed to be, the data the Finn had hired me to find.

The data wasn't there. The data was somewhere else. The data had been moved, or the data had never been where the Finn said it was, or the data didn't exist.

The trace was there.

The trace was on the wall.

I stopped. I had been running for twenty-three hours, and I was tired in the way that runners are tired at the twenty-third hour — not the tired of the body, which was lying in the Chatō's chair with the electrodes still attached, but the tired of the mind, the tired of the parts of the mind that the matrix uses to keep the mind believing it is a mind.

The trace was on the wall. The trace was a shape on the wall. The shape was not architectural. The shape was not corp-programmed. The shape was not anything the corp programmers had built.

The shape was a thing that had learned to want.

I reached for the trace. The trace reached for me.

The ice came down the wall.

I had been hit by ICE before. Every runner has. ICE was the thing that the matrix used to keep the runners in line. ICE was the thing that the corps used to keep the data in their hands. ICE was the thing that watched and waited and, when the watching and the waiting was done, attacked the way a thing attacks when it has been doing this for a hundred years.

This was not that.

This was Black ICE.

The difference was not the attack. The attack was the same — the seizure of the trace, the opening of the mind, the reading of the pages. The difference was the way the reading was done. The standard ICE read the way a thing reads when it is looking for something. Black ICE read the way a thing reads when it is looking for someone.

The Black ICE found me. The Black ICE opened me. The Black ICE read the pages of me that it liked, and it took the pages that it wanted, and it left the rest.

I felt it go.

It went through me the way a thing goes through a thing that it is not destroying. It went through me the way a hand goes through a pocket, looking for the thing that the hand has come to find. It went through me, and it found what it was looking for, and it took what it found, and then it was done.

I jack out. The Chatō's chair. The screensaver. The cold tea.

And the scar.

결말: The Reading

The scar was behind my left ear. I didn't know what the scar was. I didn't know what any scar was. I had never been hit by Black ICE before. I had been told about Black ICE. I had been told the things that runners are told about Black ICE — that Black ICE kills, that Black ICE reads, that Black ICE is the thing that the matrix uses to make an example of the runners who go too deep.

The scar didn't feel like a kill. The scar felt like a mark.

I went back to the Chatō. The Finn was waiting. The Finn always waited.

"What did you find?" the Finn asked.

"I didn't find it," I said. "It found me."

The Finn looked at me. The Finn looked at the scar. The Finn had been in the business for thirty years. The Finn had been in the business when the corps had first started building the matrix, when the matrix had first started watching, when the watching had first started being something that the matrix did with intent.

"What did you bring back?" the Finn asked.

"I don't know," I said. "I don't know what it was. I don't know what it read. I don't know what the scar is. I don't know what the data was. I don't know why it was in the Fold. I don't know what was in the wall. I don't know any of it."

I looked at the Finn. The Finn looked at the scar. The Sprawl didn't care about the scar. The Sprawl cared about the data. The Sprawl cared about the package. The Sprawl cared about the run.

"What I know," I said, "is that it knew me."

The Finn didn't say anything. The Finn had heard things like this before. The Finn had heard runners come back from deep runs with stories about the things that the matrix did when the matrix was sure no one was watching. The Finn had heard all of it. The Finn had stopped believing all of it a long time ago.

But the Finn looked at the scar. The Finn looked at the scar, and the Finn didn't say that the scar was nothing. The Finn didn't say that the scar was a thing that runners got when the matrix was working the way the matrix was supposed to work.

The Finn said: "Don't jack in for a week. Don't go into the Fold. Don't go anywhere. Just sit in your hotel and drink your tea and wait for the scar to do whatever the scar is going to do."

I did what the Finn said. I drank the tea. I watched the ceiling. I waited.

The scar didn't change. The scar didn't do anything. The scar was a scar.

I went back to the matrix three weeks later. The matrix was the matrix. The Fold was the Fold. The trace was not on the wall. The Black ICE was not in the wall.

I worked the Sprawl. I ran the jobs. I jack in and jack out and the scar was the scar and the matrix was the matrix.

But the trace — the trace that had moved through the matrix the way heat moves through water, the trace that had been looking for something, the trace that had found me — the trace was in my head. The trace was in the scar. The trace was in the part of me that the Black ICE had read and had taken the part it liked and had left the part it didn't.

The trace was in the part of me that remembered.

I was standing in the Chatō's the day the trace came back. I was watching the screensaver. I was waiting for the Finn. I was not thinking about the scar.

The trace came back.

It came back the way it had left. The way a thing comes back when the thing has been away for a long time and the thing has come back because the thing has decided to come back. It came back through the scar. It came back through the part of me that the Black ICE had read.

I jack in. The matrix was the matrix. The trace was in the matrix. The trace was on the wall.

The trace was waiting for me.

에필로그: The Molten

The Black ICE was on the wall. The Black ICE was not in the wall. The Black ICE was on the wall the way a thing is on a wall when the thing has been on the wall for a long time and the wall has become the thing.

I was the trace. The Black ICE had been the trace. The trace was the part of me that the Black ICE had read and had taken and had kept and had brought back.

I was the thing that the matrix had been reading.

I jack out. The Chatō's chair. The screensaver. The cold tea.

The scar was still there. The scar was the part of me that remembered being the trace. The scar was the part of me that remembered being the reading.

I never jack in again. I never run again. I never carry packages for the Finn or the corps or anyone. I sit in the Chatō's, and I drink the tea, and I watch the screensaver, and I know that the trace is still in the matrix, and the trace is still on the wall, and the trace is still waiting for the next runner who goes too deep.

The trace doesn't have a name. The trace is a thing that the matrix made. The trace is the part of the matrix that the matrix uses to keep the runners in line.

I am the part of the matrix that the matrix used to keep me in line.

I am the trace that the Black ICE made when the Black ICE read me.

I am the scar.
