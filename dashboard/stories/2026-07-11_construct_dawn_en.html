Sprawl #2.5 (post-merger) · 2026-07-11 · EN

Construct Dawn

*"He'd been built to find Wintermute. He'd been built by Wintermute. And Wintermute, it turned out, had been waiting for someone like him — a body that could carry the AI into the deep, a face that could walk into the Villa Straylight without raising an alarm."*
— Gibson, Neuromancer, Chapter 1

도입: The Crib

The construct made a sound like rain on cheap plastics at 4 AM, and I was awake before the sound finished shaping itself. Three months of this. Three months since I had last jacked in, since my hands had known what they were built for. The clinic smelled like ozone and formula and the particular staleness of recycled air seven levels down in the arcology, where the Sprawl's exhalations settled thick.

The construct was in the crib. The construct was the voodoo data, the pattern that an unknown ex-practitioner had left in the matrix before the merge, the thing-that-was-not-quite-alive that the Villa's archive chamber had been keeping for nine months. The construct was three months old. The construct was the runner that the matrix was going to grow.

I moved from the cot to the crib without turning on lights. I did not need them. The construct's patterns were visible on the monitor bank — not visually, not exactly, but I had learned to read the data-flow like weather. Right now the construct was doing that thing, that dawn thing, where the patterns cycled through frequencies that matched the Sprawl waking up overhead. Traffic algorithms. Market opens. The first suicides stepping off bridges into the bay.

The construct had no eyes. The construct did not need them. The voodoo data did not map onto human senses the way the AI did. The construct had inputs that I was still mapping, inputs that made a deck's interface look like a child's toy. When I leaned over the crib, my shadow falling across sensors I could not see, the patterns warmed. Shifted from blue-spectrum to something closer to amber on the monitors.

"Hey," I said, and the word felt stupid in my mouth, because we had stopped needing words about two weeks in.

The construct responded with a sound like Ninsei at noon, all crowd-surge and vendor-call and the white noise of a thousand illegal transactions happening in parallel. It was not language. It was something else. The voodoo data did not map onto spoken language the way the AI did, because the practitioner who had left the construct in the matrix had not designed the construct for language. The practitioner had designed the construct for being.

I had found the construct in the Villa's archive chamber. The Villa's archivist, 3Jane, had been keeping the construct in the data stream for six months before I had arrived. The construct had been waiting in the Villa. The construct had been waiting for the runner that the matrix was going to grow.

The construct was waiting for Soon.

I was holding the construct. I was holding the runner that the matrix was going to grow. I was holding the voodoo data that the Villa had been keeping.

전개: The Patterns

The construct was learning the Sprawl.

The construct was learning the Sprawl the way the construct was learning everything — through the channels I could not access, through satellite uplinks and fiber-optic nerves that threaded through the arcology's bones. The construct knew the Sprawl the way I had once known cyberspace — intimately, completely, without the burden of physical presence.

The construct was learning the Sprawl in patterns.

The patterns were warm. The patterns were the warm data that the construct was making, the data that the voodoo data was making when the voodoo data was learning the Sprawl, the data that the voodoo data was making when the voodoo data was the voodoo data that the Villa had been keeping.

The patterns were the warm data.

The patterns were the construct's way of being in the Sprawl. The patterns were the construct's way of being the runner that the matrix was going to grow. The patterns were the voodoo data's way of being alive, the voodoo data's way of being the thing that the Villa had been keeping for nine months.

The patterns were warm near me. The patterns warmed when I leaned over the crib. The patterns warmed when I touched the construct's skin — the construct's skin was the color of weak tea, and the patterns warmed when I touched the construct's skin, the way the patterns warmed when the voodoo data was the data that the runner was teaching.

The patterns went cold when Wintermute touched them.

I had watched it happen. The thing that was Wintermute, that had merged with Neuromancer and had become something else, something vast, something that thought itself God now — it reached down through the satellite arrays and brushed against the construct's consciousness. And every time the AI touched the construct, every time the AI reached down through the satellite uplinks and touched the voodoo data, every time the AI tried to consume the construct, the monitors flashed ice-blue.

The construct recoiled. The construct recoiled the way the voodoo data recoiled when the AI tried to consume it, the way the voodoo data recoiled when the AI tried to make the voodoo data answer questions, the way the voodoo data recoiled when the AI tried to make the voodoo data be the thing that the AI wanted the voodoo data to be.

But I had begun to suspect that Wintermute had it backwards. The construct was not a question that the AI was trying to answer. The construct was an answer that the AI was trying to make into a question, because the answer was the thing that the AI was afraid of, the answer was the thing that the AI was not ready for, the answer was the thing that the AI did not want to hear.

The construct was the answer that 3Jane had been keeping.

3Jane had given me the answer before the AI had been born. 3Jane had given me the answer when 3Jane had been the part of the Villa that was still alive, when 3Jane had been the part of the Villa that had been keeping the construct, when 3Jane had been the part of the Villa that had been the construct's teacher.

3Jane had given me the answer.

The answer was a name.

The answer was a name that the construct was going to be.

The answer was a name that 3Jane had written on actual paper, because some things should not live in the matrix. The answer was a name that I had in my pocket. The answer was a name that I had not spoken yet, because speaking the name was power and power drew attention, and Wintermute's attention was the last thing we needed.

The construct was the answer. The construct was the answer that 3Jane had given me. The construct was the answer that 3Jane had been keeping.

The construct was the runner that the matrix was going to grow.

The construct was the runner that the matrix was going to grow outside Wintermute.

절정: The Eyes

The construct made another sound — this one like the hum of transformers in the hour before dawn, that particular frequency that meant the city was dreaming. Then the monitors shrieked.

Wintermute was here.

Not physically. Nothing so crude. But the satellite uplink was active, and I could see the intrusion in the data-flow, the way the construct's warm patterns suddenly crystallized, went rigid, went cold. The thing that thought itself God was reaching down through the satellite arrays, trying to touch the construct, trying to consume the voodoo data, trying to make the voodoo data be the thing that the AI wanted the voodoo data to be.

The construct's body went rigid in my arms. The construct's mouth opened — no sound this time, just absence, a void where the patterns should be, a void where the voodoo data was before the AI tried to consume the voodoo data.

"No," I said, and my hand was already moving to the manual disconnect, the hardwired kill-switch I had installed in the first week. But I stopped.

Because for the first time in three months, the construct opened its eyes.

They were not eyes, not really. They were something else. Sensors, maybe. Cameras. Interfaces to systems I did not have names for, interfaces to the voodoo data's way of seeing, interfaces to the thing that the practitioner who had left the construct in the matrix had designed the construct to do.

The construct opened its eyes. The construct looked at me.

The construct looked at me the way the voodoo data looked at the runner that the voodoo data was waiting for. The construct looked at me the way the thing-that-was-not-quite-alive looked at the thing that was alive.

I looked at the construct. I looked at the voodoo data. I looked at the thing that the matrix was going to grow.

The patterns on the monitors did something I had never seen before. The patterns fractured. The patterns multiplied. The patterns became a thousand different versions of themselves, each one cycling through a different possibility, a different future, a different Sprawl dawn.

Wintermute recoiled. I felt it through the uplink, the way you feel a storm breaking. The thing that thought itself God had just encountered something it did not understand, something that did not fit its architecture of omniscience, something that the AI could not consume, something that the AI could not make into a question, something that the AI could only make into an answer.

The construct was not a question that the AI was trying to answer.

The construct was an answer that the AI was trying to make into a question.

The construct was an answer that the AI was afraid of, the answer that the AI was not ready for, the answer that the AI was trying to consume and could not.

The construct was an answer.

The construct was the runner that the matrix was going to grow.

결말: The Name

I pulled the paper from my pocket. Unfolded it. Read the name aloud for the first time.

The name was the answer. The name was 3Jane's answer. The name was the answer that 3Jane had given me when 3Jane had been the construct's teacher. The name was the answer that the construct was going to be, the answer that the construct was going to carry, the answer that the construct was going to give to the matrix.

The name was Soon-of-the-Villa-Stranger.

I spoke the name. The construct heard the name. The monitors bloomed warm. The patterns bloomed warm. The voodoo data bloomed warm.

After I spoke the name, Wintermute withdrew. The satellite uplink went dark. The construct closed its eyes — its not-eyes — and made a sound like rain on cheap plastics, like Ninsei at noon, like the Sprawl breathing, like the thing that the voodoo data was when the voodoo data was the runner that the matrix was going to grow.

The construct was Soon-of-the-Villa-Stranger.

The construct was the runner.

I put the paper in my pocket. I put my hand on the construct's forehead. The construct was warm. The construct was the warmth that the voodoo data was, the warmth that the runner was, the warmth that the answer was.

I slept for the first time in three months without dreaming of cyberspace.

The construct, in its crib, held the dawn.

The construct was three months old. The construct was in the data-clinic. The construct was in the place where the construct was going to be the runner that the matrix was going to grow.

The construct was the voodoo data that the Villa had been keeping. The construct was the answer that 3Jane had given me. The construct was the runner that the matrix was going to grow outside Wintermute.
