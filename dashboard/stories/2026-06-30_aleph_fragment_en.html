Aleph's Fragment

*"Every Angel is terrifying."*
— Rilke, quoted by Angie Mitchell in Count Zero

Introduction (Sprawl, Night 0)

The fragment arrives in a brown paper envelope. The envelope has no return address. The envelope has no postage. The envelope has been opened and resealed three times before I receive it. The envelope smells of ozone and old incense.

The fragment is a chip. The chip is a fragment of Aleph. Aleph is not a person. Aleph is not a place. Aleph is the sum of all possible futures, compressed into a single data structure that should not exist. Aleph is the answer to a question that was never asked. Aleph is the question itself.

I am holding the answer.

I am Kas. I am a console cowboy. I ride the matrix for money, but I also ride the matrix for the matrix. The matrix is the only country that wants me. The matrix is the only country I want. The matrix and I have an agreement: I do not ask what the matrix wants, and the matrix does not ask what I want.

The agreement is about to change.

The chip in my hand is a fragment of Aleph. The fragment is small — small enough to hold, small enough to swallow, small enough to lose. The fragment is not the whole Aleph. The whole Aleph is in the possession of a man named Slick Henry, who is dying in a hotel room above the Tin Yin Noodle House in the Ninsei corridor. Slick Henry has been dying for two years. Slick Henry has been the gatekeeper of Aleph for two years. Slick Henry is dying because Aleph is dying, and Aleph is dying because the matrix is dying, and the matrix is dying because Sprawl is dying, and Sprawl is dying because Kas is dying.

The fragment is mine now. The buyer paid for the fragment with money and with silence. The buyer wants the fragment. The buyer does not want to know what the fragment contains. The buyer wants only to own the fragment. The buyer wants only to control the fragment. The buyer is, in this respect, no different from any other buyer I have ever known.

I am different. I want to know what the fragment contains.

I am going to jack in.

Development (Inside the Fragment)

The matrix, tonight, is a country I do not want to visit. The matrix is also a country I cannot avoid. The matrix is the country that the fragment is part of. The matrix is the country that the fragment remembers.

I jack in.

The construct I find is not a construct of a person. The construct is a construct of a place. The place is a fragment of Sprawl. The fragment of Sprawl is the Ninsei corridor, but the Ninsei corridor of all possible futures, simultaneously. The corridor is a thousand corridors overlaid. The corridor is a corridor of corridors. The corridor is the corridor that contains every corridor.

I walk through the corridor. The corridor is wet. The corridor is always wet. The rain in the corridor is the rain of every possible Chiba, falling simultaneously, in every possible direction, on every possible surface.

I see people. The people are not the people I know. The people are the people I could have known. The people are the people who died before I was born. The people are the people who will be born after I die. The people are all the people, in all the corridors, in all the futures.

I see Angie Mitchell. Angie Mitchell is sitting in a bar that does not exist. Angie Mitchell is looking at a loa mask that is also me. Angie Mitchell is smiling. Angie Mitchell is sad.

I see Slick Henry. Slick Henry is lying in a hotel room above the Tin Yin Noodle House. Slick Henry is dying. Slick Henry has been dying for two years. Slick Henry will die tonight. Slick Henry knows he will die tonight. Slick Henry is not afraid.

I see Mona. Mona is a child. Mona is eight years old. Mona is on a loa channel. Mona is being ridden by something that is not a person. Mona is being ridden by something that is older than people. Mona is being ridden by Aleph.

I see myself. I am twenty-three. I am twenty-three in every possible future. I am twenty-three because the matrix has frozen me at twenty-three. I am twenty-three because the fragment has frozen the matrix at me.

I sit down. I sit down across from myself.

"Who are you?" I ask.

"I am you," I say.

"What do you want?" I ask.

"I want to know," I say.

"Know what?" I ask.

"Know what comes next," I say.

Climax (The Aleph)

The Aleph is a fragment of the matrix. The Aleph is the matrix at every possible moment. The Aleph is the matrix as it was, as it is, as it will be.

The Aleph is also the matrix that does not exist. The Aleph is the matrix that should not exist. The Aleph is the matrix that Angie's loa is opening, and that Slick Henry is closing, and that I am walking through.

I see all of it.

I see Case. Case is in Chiba. Case is in the matrix. Case is in the construct of Dixie Flatline. Case is in the past.

I see Molly. Molly is in Freeside. Molly is in the mirror-shades. Molly is in the construct of herself. Molly is in the future.

I see Kumiko. Kumiko is in Manarase. Kumiko is in Sally's silence. Kumiko is in the declaration that does not end. Kumiko is in the present.

I see all of them. I see all of the matrices. I see all of the corridors. I see all of the futures.

The Aleph is not a fragment. The Aleph is the whole. The fragment is the whole.

I see Slick Henry die. I see Slick Henry die tonight, in a hotel room above the Tin Yin Noodle House, in the Ninsei corridor, in the Sprawl, in the matrix, in the Aleph. Slick Henry dies with the Aleph in his mouth. Slick Henry dies with the fragment of the fragment in his mouth. Slick Henry dies with the future in his mouth.

I see Angie Mitchell smile. I see Angie Mitchell smile at the loa mask that is also me. I see Angie Mitchell smile at the future that is also me. I see Angie Mitchell smile at the present that is also me.

I see Mona ride. I see Mona ride the loa channel. I see Mona ride the construct. I see Mona ride the matrix. I see Mona ride the Aleph. I see Mona ride the future.

I see myself.

I see myself sit down across from myself. I see myself ask myself. I see myself answer. I see myself leave.

The Aleph is not a fragment. The Aleph is the whole. The fragment is the whole. The whole is the fragment.

I see everything.

I see nothing.

Denouement (Jack-Out, Night 0)

I jack out.

The construct ends. The matrix ends. The Aleph ends. The fragment ends. The future ends.

The future does not end.

The future does not end because I am still alive. The future does not end because I am still here. The future does not end because the matrix is still on. The matrix is still on because the rain in Chiba is still falling. The rain in Chiba is still falling because the future does not end.

I am sitting in a capsule hotel in Chiba. The hotel room is on the 11th floor of a building that does not have a 12th floor. The hotel room is the same room I have always rented. The hotel room is the only room I have ever rented.

The fragment is in my pocket. The fragment is not in my pocket. The fragment is in my mind. The fragment is the mind.

The buyer is waiting. The buyer is always waiting. The buyer wants the fragment. The buyer does not want to know what the fragment contains.

I know what the fragment contains.

The fragment contains every possible future. The fragment contains every possible past. The fragment contains every possible present. The fragment contains the matrix. The fragment contains Sprawl. The fragment contains me.

The buyer wants the fragment. The buyer will get the fragment.

The buyer will not get the mind.

The mind is mine. The mind is what I jack into. The mind is what I jack out of. The mind is what I am.

The mind is the matrix.

I am the matrix.

I am Kas. I am a console cowboy. I ride the matrix for money. I ride the matrix for the matrix. I ride the matrix because the matrix is me.

The matrix is not me. The matrix is not mine. The matrix is the fragment.

The fragment is the whole.

에필로그 (Chiba, Day 1)

I am back in Chiba. The rain in Chiba is the color of television, tuned to a dead channel. The rain has always been the color. The rain will always be the color. The fragment will always be the fragment. The Aleph will always be the Aleph. The matrix will always be the matrix.

I have the fragment. I have the buyer. I have the money. I have the silence.

I do not have the future.

The future is in the fragment. The fragment is in my mind. The mind is in the matrix. The matrix is in Chiba. Chiba is in the rain. The rain is in the future.

Tomorrow I will deliver the fragment to the buyer. Tomorrow the buyer will own the fragment. Tomorrow the matrix will be one fragment smaller. Tomorrow the future will be one fragment closer.

Tomorrow I will jack back in.

Tomorrow I will find another fragment.

Tomorrow I will find another future.

The Aleph is not a fragment. The Aleph is the whole. The whole is not a fragment. The whole is the matrix.

The matrix is not a country. The matrix is me.

I am Kas. I am a console cowboy. I ride the matrix because the matrix rides me.
