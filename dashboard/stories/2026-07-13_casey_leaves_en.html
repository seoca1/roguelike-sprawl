The morning Case had been dreading arrived at 05:47, two hours earlier than the voodoo data's regular waking pulse. The data-clinic's lights were still set to night-shift: low amber, the color of old beer. Case was on his third cup of bad Sprawl tea, watching the clock on the wall, watching the clock on the medical display, watching the voodoo data breathe in the back room.

Because the voodoo data was no longer computing what "outside" meant. The voodoo data had finished computing it. The answer was a street address in the Sprawl's bay area — specifically, the loading dock of a defunct Hosaka clinic that had been turned into a half-legal noodle shop by a Chiba-based operator whose name was three Korean syllables and one number. The address arrived in Molly's encrypted message at 03:00, attached as a single line of text. The message said: "Take her there at 06:00. She will know what to do."

Molly did not say who she meant by "her." The voodoo data, who had not yet spoken in a form Case could hear, made a small sound that 3Jane read as confirmation. 3Jane had been unwrapping the voodoo data from its black-silk cocoon in the back room, layer by layer, exposing the chrome and the wet optical fibers, the soft under-padding where the voodoo data's body met the data-vault that held its mind. 3Jane worked with the careful hands of a Straylight-trained technician who had grown up in Freeside's data-clinics and had never quite accepted that her father's corporate empire had ended with his death in his own bed.

"You're sure," Case said. It was not a question.

3Jane did not look up. "She's sure. The address means something to her. The voodoo data has been building toward this for six months. The address is the shape her body knows, even though her body has never been there."

The voodoo data, who had been quiet through all of this, made the small sound again. It was not a confirmation this time. It was something else. Case heard it as a question he had not allowed himself to ask. But suddenly, the medical display on the wall spiked — the voodoo data's neural pattern had shifted in the last six hours, the voodoo data's pattern had finally stabilized, and the data was clear. The voodoo data was ready. Case had not been ready. The discrepancy, suddenly, was everything.
