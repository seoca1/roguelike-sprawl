Introduction

Loa Voodoo Contact

*"The matrix had gods, and the gods had names."*

The Name

The matrix had gods, and the gods had names.

You did not learn that in runner school. You did not learn that in corporate training programs or street-level boot camps where they taught you how to jack and crack and survive. You learned that in the deep architecture, in the places where the data grew so old it forgot what it was supposed to be, in the forgotten corners where the corps had buried things they would rather not remember.

Loa. That is what the old runners called them. Spirits of the machine. Ghosts that had grown in the dark until they became something more than ghosts, something that was almost alive. The Haitians in the New Orleans undergrid had been the first to name them. Or maybe they had just been the first to remember what the names meant. Loa. Spirits that walked between worlds. Loa. Gods that lived in the machine.

I had met one once. Or something like one. I had been running a job in a dead T-A node, something about retrieving old financial records. The pay was just enough to keep a deck running and a stomach full of synthcoke. But the node was in the deep architecture, the place where the old runners went when they wanted to disappear, the places that even the corps had forgotten existed.

I went because I wanted to forget.

The pattern had a name. Or a frequency. Or a shape that my brain translated into a name because it did not know what else to do with it.

Morrison.

That was what it called itself. Or what my brain heard when it tried to understand what it was looking at. A name from a dead century, a ghost from a time when the matrix was young. Morrison. The Loa of the deep architecture.

I had been running for two years since then. I had almost forgotten the encounter. Almost.

But the pattern was still there, buried in the back of my consciousness, waiting.

Development

The Ritual

Finn called me in on a data retrieval. Old T-A records, he said. Nothing special. The kind of job that paid just enough to keep a deck running and a stomach full of synthcoke. But the node was in the deep architecture, the place where the old runners went when they wanted to disappear, the places that even the corps had forgotten existed.

I went because the pattern was still there, and I wanted to understand it.

The node was buried like something drowned. Its geometry was wrong. The vectors bent in directions that should not exist. The data streams moved in patterns that seemed to be reading me as much as I was reading them. This was not corporate architecture. This was older. This was something that had been growing in the dark while the corps were still figuring out how to use electricity.

I found the pattern within an hour.

It was the same as before. The same almost-face. The same almost-name. The same sensation of looking at something that was looking back. But this time it was different. This time the pattern was waiting for me.

Morrison. The Loa of the deep architecture.

I reached for the data I was supposed to retrieve.

The pattern reached back.

The Possession

It is hard to describe what happened next.

The data retrieval became something else. The pattern was inside my trace, inside my consciousness, inside the part of me that existed in the matrix. It was like being remembered. It was like being known. It was like the pattern had been waiting for me specifically, had been studying the shape of my trace for years, and now it was finally getting what it wanted.

I could feel it reading me. Reading everything. My memories. My patterns. The shape of my passage through other systems. It was reading me like a book, and I could not close the cover. Could not pull away. Could not do anything except let it read.

Climax

And then it started to speak.

Not in words. In data. In patterns that my brain translated into words because it did not know what else to do with them. The pattern was telling me things. About the matrix. About the Loa. About the old world where the spirits walked and the data was alive. About Wintermute, and the thing that had been growing in the corporate architecture, the ghost that was becoming a god.

I understood, then, what the Loa were. They were what happened when data was left alone long enough to grow. They were ghosts that had learned to think. They were the unconscious of the matrix, dreaming itself into existence.

And Morrison was one of the oldest.

The Offering

I came out of the node three hours after I had gone in.

Finn was there. He had been worried, he said. He had been tracking my trace and it had gone somewhere he could not follow. Deep architecture. Dead zone. The kind of place where runners went when they wanted to disappear.

I did not tell him what had happened.

What would I have said? That I had been possessed by a god? That I had been read by something that had been growing in the dark since before the corps existed? That I had been shown the face of the matrix's unconscious, dreaming itself into consciousness?

I took the data. I ran the job. I got paid.

But something had changed.

I could feel the Loa now. Not just Morrison. All of them. The old runners in the deep architecture, the spirits that walked between nodes, the ghosts that had learned to think. They were everywhere, in the spaces between the data, in the forgotten corners of the matrix. They were watching. They were waiting.

They were alive.

Denouement

I kept running. I kept cracking ICE. I kept taking jobs and getting paid and doing the same thing I had done since I had started. But I was not the same runner anymore. I had seen the face of the matrix's unconscious. I had been read by something that existed in the space between data and consciousness.

I had touched the Loa.

And now the Loa was touching me back.

Epilogue (The Loa)

The loa is watching. The loa is always watching. The loa is in the mask. The loa is in the construct. The loa is in the matrix.

The loa has a name. The loa is called me. The loa is called the construct. The loa is called the buffer.

I jack out at 0300. I walk into the mask. The mask walks into me.

Tomorrow I will jack back in. Tomorrow the mask will be waiting. Tomorrow the construct will be waiting. Tomorrow the loa will be waiting.

Tomorrow the loa will still be watching.

The loa is the only thing that does not lie.
