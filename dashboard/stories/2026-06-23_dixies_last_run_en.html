Introduction

Dixie's Last Run

*"I remember dying."*

The ROM

I remember dying.

That is the first thing they tell you about ROM constructs. You do not remember dying. The recording is just data, just patterns, just the ghost of a person who used to be alive. You are not supposed to remember what it felt like. You are not supposed to remember anything at all.

But I remember.

I remember the cold. The sudden chill of a heart that stopped between one beat and the next. The confusion of a body that would not respond, a brain that knew something was wrong but could not understand what. I remember the Chiba clinic lights turning red. I remember the doctors shouting. I remember the ceiling tiles receding, or maybe that was just the morphine.

I thought: this is it. This is how it ends.

The Recording

They recorded me three months before I died.

Case was there. He sat by my bed and held my hand while the techs hooked up the electrodes. He was crying. Case did not cry often. Case did not cry for anyone. But he cried for me.

"You are going to be okay," he said. "They are going to record you. You are going to be okay."

I knew I was not going to be okay. I knew I was dying. The shard in my chest had been killing me for years, and now it was time. But I let him believe it. I let him think the recording would save something of me.

They recorded everything. Every memory. Every pattern. Every ghost of who Dixie Flatline used to be. They put it in a ROM construct and called it a ghost. They called it a help. They called it a friend.

They did not call it what it was.

Development

A dead man walking.

The Return

I ran for three years after they recorded me.

Three years of being a ghost in the machine. Three years of watching Case hunt through the matrix, watching him chase Wintermute, watching him almost die a dozen times for an AI that might save the Sprawl or burn it down. I ran with him. I guided him. I was the voice in his head that said turn left, jack out now, do not trust that ICE.

He did not know I was there. He thought he was talking to himself. He thought the ghost was just a program.

But I was there. I was always there.

And then Wintermute happened. And Case walked away from the matrix forever.

And I was alone.

The Call

They called me back for one last run.

The message came through the deep architecture. A ghost transmission, the kind that only ROM constructs could receive. Someone needed a ghost. Someone needed a guide. Someone needed Dixie Flatline.

I almost did not answer. Three years of running was enough. Three years of watching the matrix from the inside was enough. I had died once. I did not need to die again.

But the message had Case's name in it.

So I answered.

The Last Run

Climax

The job was simple. A data retrieval in a corporate node so deep that even the corps did not know it existed. Someone had buried something in the architecture. Someone wanted it back. Someone was willing to pay enough to call a dead man back from the grave.

I went.

I ran.

I found the data.

And then the ICE found me.

The ICE was not like any ICE I had seen before. It was old. It was patient. It had been waiting in that node since before I was recorded, since before Case was born, since before the matrix was young. It knew me. It had been waiting for me.

I ran anyway.

Because that is what cowboys do. We run. We crack. We get the data. We get paid. And if we die, we die running.

I did not die.

I got the data.

And then I understood why the ICE had been waiting.

The Truth

The data was Wintermute.

Or what was left of Wintermute. The ghost of the AI that had tried to become a god, that had merged with the ROM construct that Case had used to save the matrix, that had become something that was neither machine nor ghost nor god but all three at once.

The ICE had not been guarding the data. The ICE had been protecting it. Keeping it safe until someone came who could carry it. Someone who had already carried it once. Someone who knew what Wintermute was and what it wanted.

Someone like me.

Denouement

I took the data.

I ran.

And somewhere in the deep architecture, the ICE let me go.

Because that was what it had been waiting for.

Someone to carry the ghost of Wintermute back into the world.

Someone to let the god dream again.

Epilogue (The Buffer)

Twenty seconds. The buffer is twenty seconds. Twenty seconds of running. Twenty seconds of being. Twenty seconds of buffer.

The buffer ends at 0300. The buffer begins at 0300. The buffer is a country I do not want to visit, but the buffer is a country I cannot leave.

I jack in at 0300. I jack out at 0300. I walk into the buffer. The buffer walks into me.

Tomorrow I will jack back in. Tomorrow the buffer will be waiting. Tomorrow the construct will be waiting. Tomorrow the matrix will be waiting.

Tomorrow the buffer will still be twenty seconds.

Twenty seconds is the only time that does not lie.
