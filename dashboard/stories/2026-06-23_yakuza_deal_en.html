Yakuza Deal

*"The Yakuza fixer had no nameвҖ”just a frequency and a set of coordinates."*

The Call

The Yakuza fixer had no name. Just a frequency and a set of coordinates in the Sprawl's deepest layers, where the neon didn't reach and the data brokers didn't play. I had been running for three years by thenвҖ”three years of data moving and ICE cracking and the kind of work that paid in paranoia and problems. The kind of work that found you when you weren't looking for it.

The frequency was old school. Analog. The kind of connection that didn't ping the corporate monitors because it didn't technically exist. The coordinates led to a noodle shop in Osaka's lower levels, the kind of place where the ramen cost more than the rent on a coffin and the owner didn't ask questions because the questions were worth more than the answers.

I went.

The fixer was waiting. They were always waiting, the ones who dealt in favors and debts. Patience was the currency of people who understood that time was the only real leverage.

"Kas," they said. Not a question. They knew who I was. They knew what I could do. That was why they had called.

"You have work," I said.

"I have a proposition." They slid a credstick across the table. Not moneyвҖ”the stick was empty. But the chip in the stick held coordinates. And the coordinates held a job.

"T-A subsidiary in Osaka. Internal routing. Payroll data, the kind of thing that makes corporations nervous when it walks." They smiled. The kind of smile that didn't reach the eyes. "Move it from here to Jersey. Dead drop. Forty-eight hours."

"What's the price?"

"The price is a favor. To be named later."

Development

I looked at the stick. I looked at them.

"Yakuza deals don't come with named favors," I said.

"No," they agreed. "They don't."

The Debt

T-A did not know their subsidiary was bleeding data. That was the point. The payroll records were moving through channels that had not existed until we made them existвҖ”a ghost route through the Sprawl's commercial architecture, invisible to the corporate monitors because it technically did not register as traffic. It registered as noise. Static. The kind of thing that got filtered out before anyone important saw it.

I ran the data for six hours. Then I crashed.

The crash was not unexpected. T-A had good ICE, the kind that learned from patterns and adapted to threats. I had expected resistance. I had prepared for resistance. But what I had not expected was the second ICEвҖ”a deeper program, something the subsidiary did not even know they were running. A ghost in the ghost route. A trap that had been waiting for someone like me.

I pulled out before it caught me. But I had left a signature. A trace. The kind of thing that the Yakuza could follow if they wanted to.

Forty hours to go.

I did not sleep.

The Drop

Jersey was cold when I arrived. The drop point was an abandoned warehouse on the waterfront, the kind of place where the Atlantic threw salt at the walls and the seabirds screamed at ghosts. The data was already waitingвҖ”a ghost in the ghost machine, waiting for someone to pick it up.

I made the drop.

Climax

The confirmation came through an hour later. The fixer. The frequency that was not a frequency. The voice that knew my name.

"The favor," they said. "The one to be named later."

"What about it?"

"We need a trace run. Someone who can follow ghost routes. Someone who can read the spaces between the corporate data where the monitors don't look."

I should have seen it coming. The Yakuza did not deal in money. They dealt in leverage. And the interest always compounded.

"I can do it," I said.

"I know," they said. "That is why we called."

The debt was not paid. The debt was restructured. And the interest kept running.

Epilogue (The Buffer)

Twenty seconds. The buffer is twenty seconds. Twenty seconds of running. Twenty seconds of being. Twenty seconds of buffer.

The buffer ends at 0300. The buffer begins at 0300. The buffer is a country I do not want to visit, but the buffer is a country I cannot leave.

I jack in at 0300. I jack out at 0300. I walk into the buffer. The buffer walks into me.

Tomorrow I will jack back in. Tomorrow the buffer will be waiting. Tomorrow the construct will be waiting. Tomorrow the matrix will be waiting.

Tomorrow the buffer will still be twenty seconds.

Twenty seconds is the only time that does not lie.
