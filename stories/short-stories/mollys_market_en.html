Molly's Market

도입

*"Molly had her priorities straight. Survival first, then the job."*

— Gibson, Neuromancer (describing Molly)

전개

Molly Millions.

She was standing at a noodle stand, her chrome-covered fingers wrapped around a bowl, her expression unreadable. The militech chrome wasn't just for show—everyone in the Sprawl knows what she did to the men who tried to take her. The results were still on display in the clinic where she had her synthetic eyes installed.

I tried to walk past. I really did.

"YOU." Her voice cut through the market noise like a knife. "Yeah, you. Come here."

I came here.

She looked me over with those synthetic eyes—the ones that could see through lies and firewalls alike. "You're a jockey," she said. "I can tell by the way you're scanning the room. Looking for exits. Looking for data." She smiled. "You've got potential."

"I'm just looking for an upgrade," I said.

절정

"Everyone's looking for something." She pushed a data chip across the counter toward me. "I'm looking for someone who can run a package to the Sprawl. No ICE, no drama. Just a simple delivery."

"What's the package?"

"None of your business." She stood, her chrome catching the neon light. "The pay is fifteen thousand. Half now, half on delivery. Interested?"

Fifteen thousand. That was more than I made in a month of safe runs.

"What do I do if I get caught?"

"You don't get caught." She leaned in close, her voice a whisper. "Because if you do, I'll find you. And I've got ways of making people talk that would make the corps jealous."

I took the chip.

"Delivery in forty-eight hours," she said. "The Finn will know where to find me."

결말

She walked away, disappearing into the market crowd like a ghost. I looked at the chip in my hand—small, innocuous, containing who knew what secrets. I could already feel the weight of it. The weight of the job. The weight of her expectations.

I headed back to the Sprawl Hole. The Finn was waiting.

"Molly's got a job for you," he said before I could speak. "I know. I already took the advance."

He handed me a credstick—five thousand, just like she said.

"Don't screw this up," he said. "Molly doesn't ask twice. And she doesn't forgive."

I pocketed the credstick and tried not to think about what was on that chip. Some things are better left unknown. But in the Sprawl, ignorance doesn't protect you. It just means you die surprised.

I started planning the run.

In the market, behind me, I could almost swear I heard her laugh.
