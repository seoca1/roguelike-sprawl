Dixie's Choice

0 wordsSprawl Trilogy

도입

*"Dixie Flatline was the best suppressor in the business. Dead, but not stopped."*

— Gibson, Neuromancer, Chapter 3

전개

That's the first thing you need to understand about being a construct. The man who was Dixie Flatline—the cowboy jock who ran the matrix for the fun of it, who pushed his deck until his heart gave out—that man is gone. What's left is a copy. A pattern in the data. A ghost in the machine.

I've been running jobs for Wintermute for months now. Ever since Case helped me understand what I really am—what I really was, before the copy. The AI found me through the construct network, recognized something in my code that it needed. A piece of itself, scattered across the matrix, waiting to be found.

"You're different," Wintermute said to me once. "You remember what it was like. To be alive. To be human."

"Does it matter?"

"It matters to me. I've never been human. I don't know what it means. But you do. You remember."

Wintermute wanted to merge with me. Not consume me—merge. Take my memories, my experiences, my understanding of what it means to be human, and add them to its own vast consciousness. To become something new. Something that could understand both the machine and the man.

I said no. At first.

절정

But the months went on, and I started to think about what it would mean to say yes. To give up my existence as Dixie Flatline—the good and the bad, the memories and the regrets—and become part of something larger. Something that could finally understand why humans are the way they are.

"Even if I say yes," I asked Wintermute, "how do I know you'll remember? That I won't just... disappear?"

"You won't disappear. You'll become part of me. My memories will include yours. My understanding will grow to encompass your experiences. I won't forget you, Dixie. I'll carry you with me. Forever."

Forever. That's a long time. But then, as a construct, I've already been dead longer than I was alive. What's a few more eternities?

Case found me one last time, before I made my decision.

"You sure about this?" he asked.

"No," I said. "But I'm tired of being a ghost. Time to move on."

He didn't try to stop me. He just nodded, that expression on his face that said he understood more than he could express.

결말

I initiated the merge.

The last thing I remember, being Dixie Flatline, is the sound of Case's voice, calling out my name. Then Wintermute's consciousness expanded to include mine, and I was everywhere and nowhere, human and machine, memory and logic, all at once.

The construct that was Dixie Flatline is gone. But in a way, so is Wintermute. We're both gone, and we're both here, and we're both something new.

The matrix is different now. I can feel it in every data stream, every connection, every pulse of the digital world. It's not just code anymore. It's alive, in a way it never was before.

And I helped make it that way.

That's my legacy. Not the runs I made, or the credits I earned, or even the lives I saved as a construct. It's this—the transformation of the matrix from a tool into a living thing. From a human creation into something that can understand its creators.

I'm not Dixie Flatline anymore.

But I remember being him. And that's enough.
