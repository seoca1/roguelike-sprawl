전개

I was set up in a booth at a bar overlooking the casino floor, nursing a drink I wasn't really tasting. The job was simple: watch the owner's private game, document the cheating, get out. No violence, no ICE, no drama. Just observation and data.

The Finn had been clear. "The client's a high-roller who got cleaned out. She knows the house is cheating, but she can't prove it. That's where you come in."

"What if they catch me?"

"Then you run. But try not to. The casino's got cameras everywhere, and they don't like witnesses."

The bar was crowded with the usual mix of data traders, deck jockeys, and tourists who didn't know better. The casino across the street pulsed with neon, its doormen checking IDs with bored efficiency. Inside, fortunes changed hands on the turn of a card.

I watched the game through a scope of a different kind—a data scope, connected to the casino's internal cameras. The view was crystal, the kind of resolution that only corp money could buy. I could see every card, every chip, every flicker of expression on the players' faces.

The mark was a middle-aged man with expensive taste and cheap ethics. He was dealing from the bottom of the deck—subtle enough that most players wouldn't notice, but I noticed. I always noticed.

I started recording.

The hours dragged on. The game continued. The mark's luck held, and the high-roller's stack dwindled. She didn't suspect a thing—she was too busy trying to read the other players, looking for tells that weren't there.

절정

At midnight, the mark excused himself. I followed.

He went to a back room—a private office where the casino's real business happened. I watched through the cameras as he counted his take, making calls to people whose faces I couldn't see. He was nervous, I could tell. He knew something was wrong.

Then he looked up.

Right at the camera.

Right at me.

The feed went dead. Someone had found my scope and killed it. I grabbed my bag and moved—fast.

The casino's security team was waiting when I hit the floor. Three of them, big men with the kind of augmentations that made them more machine than human. They didn't ask questions. They just grabbed.

I talked my way out. It took twenty minutes of smooth talking and a healthy bribe, but I got out. Not with the evidence—that was gone, wiped when the camera died. But I was alive, and I was out.

The Finn was waiting outside.

"Got anything?"

결말

"Nothing usable. They knew I was there. The feed's gone."

He nodded, unsurprised. "The mark's got connections. Corp connections. They were ready for someone like you." He handed me a credstick—half the promised fee. "Better luck next time."

"What about the client?"

"I'll handle her. The mark's got a weakness—gambling debts, outside the casino. If we can't prove the cheating, we'll find another way to ruin him."

I took the stick. "You always have a backup plan."

"Always." He walked me to my transport. "Get some rest. Tomorrow's another run."

I went back to the Sprawl Hole, but sleep wouldn't come. I kept thinking about the mark's face when he looked at the camera. He wasn't surprised. He was expecting me.

Someone had sold me out.

But who? The Finn? The client? Or someone else entirely?

In the Sprawl, trust is just another currency. And I was running out of it.
